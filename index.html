Be Prepared

For Louisiana specific information, please visit the Governor's Office of Homeland Security and Emergency Preparedness.

Hurricanes

Hurricane preparedness information for the state capital is available through the East Baton Rouge Mayor's Office of Homeland Security and Emergency Preparedness. The American Red Cross also offers information including an emergency web app and a checklist for hurricane safety.

Other safety checklists are available through the National Hurricane Center, as well as the CDC and the Department of Homeland Security.

Tornadoes

Tornado preparedness information is available through the CDC, the United States Department of Labor, as well as the Department of Homeland Security. The American Red Cross offers both a checklist and a guide to help plan before and recover after a tornado.

Thunderstorms

The U.S. Department of Commerce offers a preparedness guide for thunderstorms that includes tornado safety. The Department of Homeland Security provides a guide for staying safe during thunderstorms and addresses the dangers of lightning.

The American Red Cross offers a thunderstorm safety guide and a safety checklist.

Floods

Hurricanes, tornadoes, and thunderstorms often result in severe flooding in Louisiana. Flooding resources are available through U.S. Department of Labor, the CDC, the Department of Homeland Security, and the American Red Cross.

FEMA provides an extensive guide, including risk assessment, emergency protocols, and recovery information.
